Gathering for Honda’s Fans
Enhances Communication with Car Owners

Honda Hong Kong has always attached great importance to communication with car owners as the brand value is built upon the satisfaction of customers. In November 2021, we invited three voluntary Honda car owners to hold a gathering for fans, and even co-organized with DCH Motor Parts to enhance the synergy between us.

Starting off was the new generation of JAZZ, for which we held a car gathering for the first time, including JAZZ CROSSTAR, JAZZ EX and the hybrid version of JAZZ e:HEV. There was also a demo car equipped with a full set of "MUGEN" parts, catching the eyes of many car owners. At the event, we hosted a briefing session on the e:HEV Honda new generation hybrid system to equip car owners with information on use and maintenance. After the event was over, car owners shared the event on various social platforms, which greatly ameliorated our relationship with customers.

In addition, we invited Honda Owner Alliance (HOA) and MUGEN RR for a car gathering, with more than 100 cars participating, and the scene was stupendous. In addition to displaying Honda models of different generations, there were also the global limited edition MUGEN RR, the final edition FD2 RC and the yellow limited edition Type R FK8, satisfying the craving of hungry fans. DCH Motor Parts also launched different exquisite products of "MUGEN" and body kit discounts. Car owners were very impressed by the arrangement of this event and they had so much.

DCH Mobility Housing estate’s car-sharing service to further expand territory Wheelock's three signature housing estates to participate

Following the DCH motor leasing team's launch of a car-sharing program at The Entrance, a housing estate in Ma On Shan under CITIC Pacific in May 2021, it has been well recognized and supported by users. In the first quarter of 2022, it has reached a cooperative agreement with Harriman Property Management Limited, a subsidiary of Wheelock, and successfully expanded the service to different housing estates, enabling more residents to enjoy a convenient and environmentally friendly experience of shared driving.

The first phase of the car-sharing service at Harriman Estate will be implemented at THE PARKSIDE and MONTEREY in Tseung Kwan O, and NAPA in Tuen Mun. Residents will only need to download the mobile app, and the entire car rental process can be completed automatically, easily and quickly. The electric vehicles provided can also contribute to the team's endeavour in environmental protection and sustainable development. In the future, the team will expand the car-sharing service to different domains such as large shopping malls, public parking lots and commercial facilities, making the car-sharing service within grasp and the service will be made available all across Hong Kong.

DCH Mobility Contributes during the Pandemic

The shared pick-up service for airport employees provided by DCH Mobility not only provides a new transportation solution for airport employees, but also provides assistance in controlling the pandemic. This ensures that Hong Kong’s citizens, healthcare workers, medical institutions, employers and schools can get test kits faster and more efficiently so as to fight the pandemic together. The team is committed to undertaking the mission of serving the society and fulfilling corporate social responsibility, in hopes that the public can resume normal life as soon as possible.

DCH Garnered Awards at the
"1st Technical Contest for Automobile Maintenance Industry in Bao'an District, Shenzhen"

The motor industry promotes the construction of talent teams and provides strong support for the scientific and harmonious development of the industry. The 1st Technical Contest for Automobile Maintenance Industry in Bao'an District, Shenzhen, led by Shenzhen Bao'an Vocational and Technical School, guided by the Bao'an Administration of Shenzhen Transportation Bureau, organized by Shenzhen Bao’an District Automobile Maintenance Industry Association, and undertaken by Shenzhen Bao’an Vocational and Technical School and Shenzhen Fenda Vocational and Technical School, was held on 3rd and 4th December last year. A total of 20 teams from two provinces participated in the competition, which incorporated the teacher division, the student division and the worker division.
