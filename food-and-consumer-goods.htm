Château Grand Mayne Saint-Émilion Grand Cru Class 2015─  Famous both at Home and Abroad and a Frequent Winner of Awards 

Located at the West of St. Emillion, the oldest area in Bordeaux, France, Château Grand Mayne is adjacent to the vineyards of the famous winery - Chateau Caillou. The winery has a history of over 5 decades .

The deceased Baron Philip de Rothschild, owner of Château Mouton Rothschild (one of the five Premier Grand Cru in Bordeaux), tasted the Chateau Grand Mayne Saint-Emilion 1955 at a restaurant in Belgium and immediately exchanged the wine with a few boxes of Château Mouton Rothschild 1955, mirroring the excellence and value of the Château Grand Mayne premium wine. 

Today, the winery has won several awards on multiple occasions for its fine wines, and the 2015 vintage even garnered an impressive score of 92 or above from various world-renowned wine critics, including Robert Parker and James Suckling.

Miyoshikiku Jyunmai Daiginjyo – An Exquisite Brewing 

Miyoshikiku Brewery was founded in 1889. Produced with strictly selected Yamada-Nishiki, king of sake-rijst (the rice used for sake brewing), the first-class soft fukuryusui water from Yoshino River, one of Japan's three most famous rivers, and Tokushima’s yeast. Products from Miyoshikiku Brewery have a gorgeously floral fragrance with a scent of fresh fruit. The taste is fresh and smooth! 

Miyoshikiku Jyunmai Daiginjyo has always been the best-seller since DCH started the distribution last year. Beside using Yamada-Nishiki from Tokushima with seimaibuai level of 40%, the method of "Fukuro-tsuri" (Drip-sake) method is also employed. 

"Fukuro-tsuri”  is made from only drops by gravity from moromi (fermentation mush) in a hanged filter bag. No pressing by pressure. The elegant fragrance and fascinating taste are created only from drops. Since the filtration method is time-consuming with sacrce output, “Fukuro-tsuri” is generally used for high-end wines. The sake produced can retain more original flavours, accentuating the wine’s fragrance or rich texture. The taste is sweet, delicate, with a sense of purity with smooth texture. This is a dreamy masterpiece among sakes. 

Products Details:
Origin: Japan 
Type: Sake 
Seimaibuai Level: 40% (Jyunmai Daiginjyo) 
Rice: Yamada-Nishiki
